London Auction Market Trends: What to Expect in 2025

September 10, 2025 Market Analysis By James Wilson

The London property auction market has always been a bellwether for the wider UK property sector. As we move through 2025, several distinct trends are emerging that savvy investors should be aware of. Our analysis of recent auction data, combined with economic forecasts and policy changes, reveals a complex but opportunity-rich landscape for auction property investors in the capital.

Market Overview: A Return to Stability

After the volatility of the early 2020s, the London auction market has settled into a more predictable pattern. Transaction volumes have stabilized at approximately 4,500 properties per year across the major auction houses, with an average lot size of £750,000 for residential properties and £1.2 million for commercial lots.

The pandemic-driven exodus from London has largely reversed, with strong demand returning to central locations. However, this demand is now more discerning, with buyers showing clear preferences for specific property types and locations.

London auction transaction volumes by quarter, 2023-2025

Key Trend #1: The Rise of Mixed-Use Properties

One of the most significant trends we're observing is the increasing premium being paid for mixed-use properties. Buildings with commercial space on the ground floor and residential units above are commanding 15-20% higher prices compared to similar single-use properties.

This trend is being driven by several factors:

  • Planning Flexibility: Recent changes to permitted development rights have made it easier to convert parts of mixed-use buildings between commercial and residential use.
  • Income Diversification: Investors value the ability to generate income from multiple sources within a single asset.
  • Urban Regeneration: Local authorities are increasingly favoring mixed-use developments in planning decisions.

Areas seeing particularly strong demand for mixed-use properties include Hackney, Southwark, and Waltham Forest, where local planning policies actively encourage this type of development.

Key Trend #2: Sustainability Premium

Properties with high energy efficiency ratings or clear potential for energy improvements are now commanding a significant premium at auction. Our data shows that residential properties with an EPC rating of B or above are selling for 8-12% more than comparable properties with lower ratings.

This trend is being accelerated by:

  • Regulatory Changes: The requirement for rental properties to have a minimum EPC rating of C by 2025 (for new tenancies) is driving investor demand for energy-efficient properties or those that can be easily upgraded.
  • Energy Cost Concerns: Despite recent stabilization, energy costs remain a significant consideration for occupiers.
  • ESG Investment Criteria: Institutional investors are increasingly bound by ESG criteria that favor sustainable assets.
"We're seeing a clear bifurcation in the market. Properties with good energy performance are attracting competitive bidding, while those requiring significant investment to meet minimum standards are selling at discounts of 10-15%, reflecting the cost of necessary upgrades." - Sarah Jenkins, Director at Allsop Auctions

Key Trend #3: Outer London Hotspots

While central London has recovered, some of the most interesting auction opportunities are emerging in outer London boroughs. Areas benefiting from transport improvements, particularly those on the Elizabeth Line (Crossrail), continue to outperform the wider market.

Our analysis identifies these outer London auction hotspots for 2025:

1. Bexley

With average residential prices still below £400,000 and strong rental demand, Bexley is attracting significant investor interest. Properties here are achieving 95-98% of their open market value at auction, compared to the London average of 85-90%.

2. Barking and Dagenham

Major regeneration projects and improved transport links are transforming this historically undervalued borough. Auction lots here are seeing some of the most competitive bidding in London, with an average of 12 bidders per lot compared to the London average of 7.

3. Croydon

Despite setbacks in some major development projects, Croydon's auction market is showing remarkable resilience. The borough's connectivity and relative affordability continue to attract investors, particularly for lots with permitted development potential.

Year-on-year price growth in London boroughs (auction sales, 2024-2025)

Key Trend #4: The HMO Opportunity

Houses in Multiple Occupation (HMOs) are emerging as a particularly strong performing segment in London auctions. Despite regulatory challenges, the yield premium they offer is attracting experienced investors.

Licensed HMOs are typically achieving gross yields of 7-9% in London, compared to 4-5% for standard buy-to-let properties. This yield gap is driving competitive bidding for properties with existing HMO licenses or those suitable for conversion.

However, this market is increasingly sophisticated, with clear preferences for:

  • Properties with existing, compliant licenses
  • Locations within walking distance of transport hubs
  • Areas with strong professional or student populations

Boroughs with pragmatic approaches to HMO licensing, such as Lewisham and Greenwich, are seeing particularly strong demand for this property type.

Key Trend #5: Commercial to Residential Conversion Opportunities

The structural shift in office and retail demand continues to create opportunities for conversion to residential use. Recent changes to permitted development rights have expanded the range of commercial properties that can be converted without full planning permission.

Our analysis shows that:

  • Former office buildings suitable for conversion are selling at 20-30% premiums to their commercial value
  • Retail units with residential upper parts are particularly sought after
  • Secondary retail parades in residential areas offer some of the best value-add opportunities

However, investors need to be increasingly selective, as local authorities are becoming more restrictive with Article 4 Directions that remove permitted development rights in certain areas.

Challenges and Risk Factors

While opportunities abound, several risk factors could impact the London auction market in 2025:

Interest Rate Sensitivity

Although interest rates have stabilized, they remain historically high. Any further increases could significantly impact investor calculations, particularly for leveraged buyers.

Regulatory Changes

The rental reform legislation is creating uncertainty in the buy-to-let sector. Properties heavily dependent on rental income may see valuation impacts as the full implications of these changes become clear.

Construction Cost Inflation

While moderating from recent peaks, construction costs remain elevated. This is particularly relevant for properties requiring significant renovation or conversion.

Auction Strategy for 2025

Based on our analysis, we recommend the following strategies for London auction investors in 2025:

1. Focus on Value-Add Opportunities

Properties where value can be added through relatively straightforward improvements (energy efficiency upgrades, layout optimization, planning enhancements) offer the best risk-adjusted returns.

2. Be Borough-Specific

Different London boroughs have vastly different approaches to planning, licensing, and enforcement. Success increasingly depends on deep local knowledge rather than general London expertise.

3. Consider Joint Ventures

For larger projects, joint ventures between investors with complementary skills (financing, planning, construction) are becoming increasingly common and successful.

4. Leverage Data

The most successful auction buyers are those with access to comprehensive data on comparable sales, planning precedents, and local market dynamics. This is where services like Auction Insights provide a competitive edge.

Conclusion

The London auction market in 2025 offers a wealth of opportunities for informed investors. While headline prices have stabilized, the potential for outperformance through strategic property selection and improvement remains substantial.

The key to success lies in granular analysis, borough-specific knowledge, and a clear understanding of the value-add potential of each lot. By focusing on the trends identified in this analysis, investors can position themselves to identify and secure the most promising opportunities in London's dynamic auction market.
